How to Make Butter Chicken

History of Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken, also known as "Murgh Makhani," is one of the most famous dishes in Indian cuisine. The dish originated in Delhi, India, in the 1950s, at a restaurant called Moti Mahal. The story goes that the chefs at Moti Mahal accidentally created the dish by mixing leftover tandoori chicken with a rich, creamy tomato-based sauce, which was then served to customers.

Over time, Butter Chicken became a staple of Indian cuisine, renowned for its rich flavor and creamy texture. It is often served with naan bread or rice and has become a beloved dish not only in India but around the world. Butter Chicken is typically made with chicken marinated in yogurt and spices, then cooked in a flavorful sauce made with butter, tomatoes, and cream.

Today, Butter Chicken can be found in Indian restaurants worldwide, and it remains one of the most iconic dishes of Indian cuisine.

Butter Chicken Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into cubes
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (optional, for spice)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Warm naan bread or basmati rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a bowl, combine the yogurt, half of the ginger, garlic, cumin, coriander, turmeric, paprika, and salt. Add the chicken cubes and mix until well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.
  2. Cook the chicken: Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the marinated chicken and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.
  3. Make the sauce: In the same pan, add butter and saut√© the chopped onion, garlic, and the remaining ginger until softened, about 3-4 minutes. Stir in the garam masala and chili powder (if using), cooking for another minute.
  4. Prepare the butter chicken sauce: Add the crushed tomatoes and sugar to the pan. Stir well, and let the sauce simmer for 10-15 minutes until it thickens slightly. Add the heavy cream, and simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  5. Combine the chicken with the sauce: Add the cooked chicken to the sauce, stirring to coat. Let it cook for another 5 minutes, ensuring the chicken is fully coated and heated through.
  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve the butter chicken with warm naan bread or basmati rice. Enjoy!
